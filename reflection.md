# Palmetto Coast High Leadership Academy — Reflection

## REFLECTION

The Palmetto Coast High Leadership Academy case study represents the most comprehensive project I have completed thus far.

Unlike my previous case studies, which focused primarily on designing and developing professional websites for local businesses, this project required consideration of long-term content management, deployment workflows, maintainability, and administrative usability.

The result is not a simple static website, but a complete digital system that allows authorized staff to manage content without modifying the underlying codebase.

## WHAT WENT WELL

The planning phase proved invaluable throughout the project. Defining the information architecture, CMS workflow, content model, and repository structure before implementation significantly reduced complexity during development.

Integrating Decap CMS with GitHub OAuth was one of the project's strongest outcomes. Although the implementation required additional research and refinement, it ultimately produced a workflow that allows authorized users to update the website through a familiar administrative interface while preserving a Git-based version history.

The overall architecture also remained consistent throughout development. Separating content from presentation, organizing documentation, and maintaining a clear project structure made the website easier to develop, review, and maintain.

## WHAT I LEARNED

This project reinforced that building a successful digital system extends far beyond writing HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

Real-world websites often require thoughtful consideration of deployment, authentication, content management, documentation, accessibility, and long-term maintenance. Each of these components contributes to the overall reliability and sustainability of the system.

The project also demonstrated the value of comprehensive documentation. Recording design decisions, implementation details, and reflections throughout development created a much clearer understanding of the reasoning behind the final product.

## KEY TAKEAWAYS

- Careful planning simplifies complex implementations.
- Content management should be designed around the people maintaining the website.
- Separating content from presentation improves long-term maintainability.
- Documentation is an essential part of software development, not an afterthought.
- Building digital systems requires consideration beyond frontend development alone.

## CLOSING

This case study represents a significant milestone in my development as a systems builder.

It demonstrates my ability to design, develop, document, and deploy a maintainable digital system that balances usability, accessibility, and long-term sustainability while supporting the practical needs of a real-world organization.
